Charminar, one of the most famous historical monuments of Telangana is a delightful blend of both ancient and modern sculpture and architecture. The impressive symbol of Hyderabad, Charminar was constructed in 1591, by Mohammed Quli Qutab Shah to commemorate the end of the plague in the city.Â It is an impressive square monument with four minarets. The arch is illuminated daily in the evening, an unforgettable sight indeed. The monument is a magnificent square edifice of granite, built upon four grand arches facing North, South, East and West. These arches support two floors of rooms and gallery of archways. At each corner of the square structure is a minaret rising to a height of 24 meters, making the building nearly 54 meters tall. It is these four (char) minarets (minar) that give the building, its name 'Charminar'. Each minar stands on a lotus-leaf base, a special recurrent motif in Qutub Shahi buildings. The Charminar is about 7 km from Hyderabad railway station. It is 5 km from Hyderabad bus station. Excellent private transport is available from all parts of the twin-cities. Called the "Arc De Triumph of the East", Charminar symbolizes Hyderabad. As old as the city itself, the four imposing towers of this edifice stand in the heart of the old city as a hallmark of the Qutub Shahi era. The nearby tourist spots include: Bhagyalakshmi Temple Mecca Masjid
