"" Ajanta and Ellora caves, considered to be one of the finest examples of ancient rock-cut caves, are located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra, India. Many visitors across the world visit these caves because of resemblance of Indian natural architecture. While the Ajanta caves have some of India's most sophisticated ancient paintings, the Ellora caves are renowned for their extraordinary architecture. Though considered together, in real, Ajantha and Ellora are two different caves each consisting a number of caves. Ajantha with a total of 29 caves built between the 2nd century BC and 6th century AD, whereas the Ellora caves are more spread out and have a total of 34 caves which dates back to 6th and 11th century AD. The Ajantha and Ellora are sacred sites and are designated by UNESCO as World Heritage sites. These caves are a mixture of Hindu, Buddhism and Jain religions depicting the diversity in their structures. One of the most famous places in the entire Ajanta and Ellora Caves is the Kailash Temple, which is also the single most massive monolithic structure globally. These rock-cut caves containing carvings are some of the best examples of ancient Indian architecture and sculpture. You can travel from your city or country to the Aurangabad airport by air. After that, the caves stand at a distance of 100 kms from the airport. The closest transportation service can be found via the Aurangabad railway stations. The caves of Ellora are only at a 45 minutes distance while those in Ajanta are 1.5 hours away. You can travel from Mumbai till Aurangabad in just 6 or 7 hours. Several roads and highways connect Aurangabad to the neighbouring cities including Mumbai, Ahmedabad and Hyderabad. You may take a bus or travel in your car all the way to the caves. The nearby tourist attractions are Ajantha Visitor center, which has five museum halls. Grishneshwar Temple, Ellora The Buddhist Caves, Ellora """
